March 2024 Print / Process

The first print
experiment

We pulled the first screen at 4pm, just as the light shifted from direct to diffuse. The ink was slightly too thick. The edges bled by about 2mm on the left side. We printed twenty sheets and kept three.

The three we kept were not the cleanest. They were the ones where the imperfection felt like it belonged to the composition — where the bleed was not a mistake but a decision the print had made for itself.

That afternoon set something. We stopped trying to eliminate error and started trying to understand which errors were worth keeping.

April 2024 Material

Testing
paper stock

Twelve paper stocks tested over two weeks. Coated surface: ink sits on top, too precise. Uncoated 120gsm: absorbs unevenly in humidity. The 170gsm natural — slightly cream, slightly rough — takes ink the way linen takes dye. It resists slightly. That resistance produces something.

April 2024 Workspace

Workspace
observation

The studio is a 10×12 foot room with one east-facing window. By 10am, direct light. By noon, the light goes diffuse and slightly green from the tree outside. We have stopped adjusting for this. We work in the green light and photograph in it. It is the light we have.

May 2024 Process

Late night
process

11pm. The city goes quiet enough to hear the fan. We mix inks by weight, not by eye — but tonight we mixed by eye and got it wrong. The colour came out three tones warmer than intended.

We ran twenty sheets anyway. Twelve came out. The warmth was the best thing about them.

There is a version of this work that happens in daylight, precisely, with measurements. That version produces better results on the first attempt. This version, the one that happens when you are tired and working by instinct, produces something else — something we cannot plan for and cannot repeat.

We are trying to understand the difference between the two, and when each one is appropriate.

Observation — No Image

On the cost of
refinement

We spent six hours refining a layout today. The first version, made in twenty minutes, was the strongest. We cannot explain why we kept going. Habit, probably — the assumption that more time produces better work. It does not always.

The discipline we are trying to build is knowing when to stop. The work tells you. You have to be quiet enough to hear it.

July 2024 Typography

Character spacing
as architecture

Three hours setting type. The same twelve words in eight configurations. Letter-spacing as the variable. The wider it goes, the more monumental — and the less human. We settled at 0.08em. Just beyond neutral.

August 2024 Material

Canvas, before
printing

Natural canvas arrives greige — not white, not cream. In afternoon light it reads as warm. Under a phone camera it reads as cold. We stopped photographing material under phone cameras. The light has to be real.
