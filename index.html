Philosophy

We make things
that hold still
under attention.

Good design does not announce itself. It settles into use, into daily rhythm, into the peripheral vision of living. We are drawn to work that resists the compulsion to perform.

Our reference points are long-lived: a Muji notebook, a Leica body, a weathered wooden shophouse on Jalan Tuanku Abdul Halim. Objects and systems that accumulate meaning slowly.

We believe restraint is a form of generosity. Taking less space allows more room for thought.

Based in Southeast Asia  ·  Working across print, screen, and space

Process

01

Listening

Before anything else, we try to understand what already exists. The existing language, rhythm, and constraints of a project are its most honest brief.

02

Reduction

We remove until removal would cause damage. Each decision is held up to a simple question: does this serve the work, or does it serve an idea of the work?

03

Material

We work with the texture of each medium — screen, paper, or space. Fidelity to material is not a limitation but the source of character.

04

Release

Work is finished when the maker becomes unnecessary. We hand things over with enough room for the work to be used, lived with, and quietly forgotten.
