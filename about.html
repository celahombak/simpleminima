About the Studio

A studio
that makes
quietly.

01
Who We Are

A small studio
in Cyberjaya

SimpleMinima is an independent creative studio based in Cyberjaya, Malaysia. We make physical objects — printed posters, garments with small marks, documentation — and we take the making seriously.

The studio was founded on a single conviction: that the process of making is as valuable as what gets made. That documentation is not marketing. That restraint is not laziness. That the work should carry evidence of real hands.

We are not a startup. We do not scale. We run in batches, by hand, and we close runs when they are done.

02
Philosophy

We make things
that resist velocity

Everything we design begins with a question: what is the quietest version of this that is still true? The answer is rarely the first version. It is usually something arrived at after removing things — a colour, a line, a word — until what remains cannot be reduced further.

Minimalism, as we practice it, is not an aesthetic preference. It is a discipline of attention. To make less requires understanding more. The constraints are not decorative — they are the work.

We are also not neutral. The studio has a palette, a typography system, a set of materials it refuses to use. These are positions, not rules. They come from years of looking at things and deciding, quietly, what we believe.

03
Physical Objects

Why physical
objects still matter

We live in a time when everything can be made instantly and discarded just as fast. The physical object has become strange again — something that resists duplication, that occupies space, that develops a patina through use.

A screen-printed poster on uncoated paper is not just paper. It is a specific ink, a specific mesh count, a specific pressure, a specific room. It cannot be exactly replicated. That specificity is not a limitation — it is the point.

When someone buys something from SimpleMinima, they are not buying a product. They are acquiring a moment of attention.

04
Process & Documentation

Documentation
as practice

We photograph and write about what we make — not to market it, but because the record matters. The journal entries are not crafted narratives. They are field notes: this happened, this failed, this surprised us.

Process documentation is a form of honesty. It shows the gap between intention and result. It shows that the studio is not a machine producing perfect objects — it is a place where someone is trying to figure something out, in real time, in a particular light.

05
Place & Atmosphere

Southeast Asian
light & humidity

Cyberjaya is not a neutral backdrop. The light here is particular — diffuse, warm, slightly overexposed at certain hours. The humidity changes how ink dries. The heat changes how you move through a workspace. The afternoon rain changes what you think about.

The studio works with these conditions rather than against them. We do not wish for a cooler, drier, more northern light. The imperfections that tropical air introduces into our materials are part of the visual identity — they are where the work is from.

Our palette, our typography weight, our choice of uncoated stock — all of it has been shaped, slowly, by being in this place and paying attention to what it asks of us.

"We are a small studio. We make things with care, in small numbers, in a specific place. That is enough."
