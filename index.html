Philosophy

We make things slowly,
on purpose, with
hands that remember
the material.

SimpleMinima is not a brand in the conventional sense. It is a way of working — an orientation toward objects, process, and documentation that resists the velocity of modern production.

We believe in imperfection that carries evidence. In the faint ink bleed on the edge of a screen-printed poster. In the crease of paper that has been held too long. In the photograph taken at the wrong hour of the day, in the wrong light, which somehow tells a truer story.

Our work begins with observation. It ends — if it ends — when the thing we are making has found its quietest form.

How We Work

Process
as Evidence

01
Observation

Nothing begins in the studio. It begins outside — in the texture of a painted wall, the angle of afternoon light through louvres, the particular stillness of a hardware shop at 2pm. We document before we make.

02
Experimentation

The first version is almost always wrong. We allow that. Paper tests, ink tests, layout tests — each failure is a record of what the material refuses to be.

03
Screen Printing

Ink pulled across mesh, pressed into paper or fabric. The registration is never perfect. We have stopped trying to make it so. The slight misalignment is where the handmade lives.

04
Photography

We photograph everything — the workspace, the failures, the material before it is worked. The images are not marketing. They are a record that we were here, doing this, at a specific moment.

05
Slow Refinement

We do not rush to release. Some objects sit for weeks before they are considered done. Some are never released. The archive holds as much as the shop.
